The European Travel Compromise

Every day, 55,000 people cross EU borders, and 75% are ready for sustainable options. But today's travel forces an impossible choice. We're told to pick one: speed, sustainability, or convenience. Why can't we have all three?

Chapter 1: The Speed Trap

We're offered a false choice: fly at a blistering 839 km/h, or crawl on a conventional train at 150 km/h. While 70% of people prefer trains, the speed is simply not competitive for international travel, forcing a compromise before the journey even begins.

Average Speed (km/h)

Chapter 2: The Carbon Cost

The "fast" option comes at a massive environmental price. Flying produces an enormous amount of CO2 per kilometer, completely undermining European sustainability goals. The train is incredibly clean, but it's too slow. We are forced to choose between our planet and our time.

CO2 Emissions (g/km per passenger)

Chapter 3: The Waiting Game

A plane's "speed" is misleading. The data shows a staggering 120 minutes of "access time"‚Äîthe hassle of check-in, security, and boarding. This high-friction experience wastes time before you ever leave the ground. Train and bus travel are simple and efficient to board, but they lose all that time on the journey itself.

Average Access Time (Minutes)

Chapter 4: The Capacity Constraint

Even if we accept their flaws, buses and planes simply can't move enough people. With a capacity of only 40 and 180 passengers, they are inefficient solutions for a connected Europe. The train system is the only one built for mass transit, with a capacity of 800, but it lacks the speed to make it the default choice.

Passenger Capacity

Our Impossible Choice

We are forced to choose between our time and our values. This is the compromise we reject.

The Plane: Fast & Flawed

You get speed (839 km/h) but you pay with your time (120 min access) and your conscience (154g CO2/km).

The Train: Green & Slow

You get sustainability (3g CO2/km) and capacity (800) but you sacrifice your time (150 km/h avg speed).

The Bus: The Long Haul

You get simplicity (20 min access) but lose on every other metric: speed (80 km/h) and capacity (40).

The Solution: 1 Track, 1 Train, 2 Systems

HMR ends the compromise. We are not building a new network; we are upgrading the one we already have. Our solution is built on two core principles:

The Retrofitted Train

A high-speed train that operates normally at low speeds, then supports maglev levitation upon reaching over 200 km/h, achieving 500 km/h on upgraded tracks.

The "Plug-and-Play" Track

A modular upgrade for existing tracks. This module supports our maglev trains while still allowing all conventional trains to pass over it without issue.

A True Hybrid System

Old trains and new trains on the same track. This is the key to a realistic, cost-effective, and scalable rollout across all of Europe.
